Problem

Cross-currency payments settle at the naive spot rate

Stablecoins drift from the currency they're pegged to. Nobody prices that gap before paying — so payers quietly overpay, every time.

30–50 bps
typical EURC deviation from its euro peg, observed live on Arc via Pyth.
~$3,000
left on the table on a single €1M B2B settlement at 30 bps.
  • Remittances, marketplace payouts, global payroll — all convert at spot.
  • As AI agents start settling autonomously, this leakage scales with them.
FX-aware Settlement Agent2

Solution

The agent prices the route before it pays

  • Reads three live Pyth feeds on Arc — EUR/USD (FX), EURC/USD, USDC/USD — with confidence + staleness on every read.
  • Computes the basis: how far EURC trades from its own euro peg.
  • Routes the cheaper way: buy discounted EURC on-chain, or settle direct in USDC when there's no edge.
  • Settles in USDC via a Circle Developer-Controlled Wallet — server-side signing, no private keys in the app — with a memo for reconciliation.
  • Charges a sub-cent nanopayment per settlement for the routing service.
Safety by design: if the oracle is stale or its confidence is wide, the agent refuses the FX bet and settles safely in USDC.

Where this goes

From hackathon to something I keep running

  • Depth-aware confidence — size the trade against pool depth and expected slippage, not just price confidence.
  • More corridors — GBP and JPY feeds are already live on Arc; StableFX partner stablecoins as they land.
  • KRW1 end-to-end — settle into the won stablecoin itself once it's on Arc, closing the corridor.
  • Agent-to-agent — the agent as a paid routing service other agents call, billed per settlement.
Thesis: stablecoin FX dislocations are real, Arc makes them programmable, and an agent that prices the route before it pays turns that into savings — autonomously, per payment.
